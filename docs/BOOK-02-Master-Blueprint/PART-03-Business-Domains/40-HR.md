# HR

> *"HR manages people operations inside an organization."*

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines the HR domain blueprint.

HR supports internal people operations such as employee records, onboarding, roles, documents, approvals, and internal workflows.

---

# Overview

HR is distinct from Identity.

Identity controls platform access.

HR manages employee-related business information and people operations.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The HR domain may own:

- Employee records.
- Onboarding workflows.
- HR documents.
- Time-off requests.
- Approvals.
- Internal policies.
- Employee lifecycle.
- HR analytics.

---

# HR Flow

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    Candidate[Candidate] --> Employee[Employee]
    Employee --> Onboarding[Onboarding]
    Onboarding --> Active[Active Employee]
    Active --> Changes[Role or Policy Changes]
    Changes --> Offboarding[Offboarding]
```

---

# AI Opportunities

AI may assist by:

- Drafting onboarding plans.
- Answering policy questions.
- Summarizing employee records.
- Routing HR requests.
- Generating internal documents.

---

# Security Considerations

HR data is highly sensitive.

Access should be tightly controlled, audited, and separated from general workspace visibility.

---

# Key Takeaways

- HR manages people operations.
- HR is different from Identity.
- HR data is sensitive and requires strong controls.
- HR workflows often involve approvals.
